# UNE Student Sync Prototype

This prototype lets students choose LMS units and content types, then generate a seven-day offline workbook view. It simulates syncing assessment and contact-time data into a simple planning flow with printable A4/A3 output and CSV export for Excel. It is designed to reduce repeat LMS checking through a clearer, lightweight study-planning interface. It also has the option to synchronise with Gmail/Outlook, and send notification emails if assessments change.

## Run
Open `index.html` in a browser.

## Stack
- HTML
- Bootstrap
- JavaScript

## MoSCoW

### Must Have
- ✅ Works even without staff involvement, making it resilient.
- ✅ Combines digital automation and physical/printable outputs.
- ✅ Calendar integration improves planning and visibility.
- ✅ Works offline.
- ✅ Flexible: students choose exactly what to pull from the LMS.
- ✅ Simple visual cues reduce cognitive load.
- ✅ No hidden options for better visibility.

### Should Have
- ✅ Reduces missed deadlines by centralising sync choices.
- ✅ Students are already using Outlook, Google Calendar and Excel, showing a clear market for external integration.
- ✅ Personalised visual layout design.
- ✅ Could integrate with calendars, summaries, or study tools.
- ❌ Progress tracking stimulates active planning.
- ✅ Reduce repeated visits to the LMS.
- ✅ Differentiate between similar events at a glance.
- ✅ Add intuitiveness to existing Ui.
- ✅ Integration with EXCEL or other proffessional.
- ✅ Aligns with current behaviour of students maintaing paper diaries.

### Could Have
- ❌ Checklist feature gives  sense of control.
- ✅ Avoids digital clutter.
- ❌ Public dashboard builds transparency and shared accountability.
- ❌ Crowdsourced confirmations increase accuracy over time.
- ✅ Timestamp and changelog provide students with trust of the data.
- ✅ Immediate guidance at the right moment.
- ✅ Stronger sense of place within institutional tool.
- ✅ Simple guided setup.
- 🟡 Can evolve into a full automation hub for academic workflows.
- ✅ Students' manual Excel sheets and paper diaries provide a ready made list of the specific data points they value most.
- ✅ Works offline, allowing students to use it when LMS is blocked or inaccessible.

### Won't Have
- Limited functionality as a low-fidelity prototype.
- Initial setup may feel time-consuming.
- Get unnoticed.
- Once printed, the data becomes static. If data changes mid week, may be outdated until next print.
- Low participation could weaken perceived reliability.
- LMS changes could disrupt scraping or data collection.
- Being perceived as over-engineered for basic student needs.
- Repetitiveness could generate noise.
- Over-reliance on scores could reduce critical checking by students.
- Design changes could break clarity.
- LMS layout or access changes may break syncing.
- Adds an extra tool.
- Harder to scan and understand quickly.
- Data inconsistency if tutors don't update the offical system.
- Single point of failure.
